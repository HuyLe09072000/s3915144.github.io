In topic 4, Dr. Jonathan Crellin guided students to develop technical report writing structures that support science and engineering disciplines. Furthermore, the structures formed will be a key element in supporting the presentation exercise and the future discipline. He presents the keys to leading pupils with clear goals, substance, and analysis for essays and presentations, based on his extensive expertise. Students took an extensive look at the audience's standard, compared slides to get a solid idea of the situation and meticulously prepared presentations. Interaction with the audience is also an important factor mentioned at the end of the lesson.

Topic 3 had a very vibrant classroom atmosphere, and the students enthusiastically engaged in all of the activities led by lecturer Peter Farrell. The practice session at the end of the Thursday session assisted students in mastering and developing what they had learned. Furthermore, instructor Jonathan Crellin's online lesson was chock-full of new information to pique students' interest with vivid comparison examples. When students work together to accomplish group activities and communicate in between individual exercises, they have a very professional attitude. This has been a very successful week for the students' receptivity and the full transmission of the teachers.

The knowledge I gained from the two courses was tremendously beneficial since it reinforced my deficiencies from the prior UniStart class and gave me greater confidence in presenting logical arguments. In addition, I can quickly synthesize and produce ideas from a large amount of data from a range of sources. Not only did I improve my ability to express myself clearly, but I also learned more about the conventional framework for technical essays in Lesson 4. In science and technology, presentations are critical because students must be able to comprehend facts and transmit them to an audience without becoming bored, as well as enable them to develop a strong sympathy for the individual presentation. For example, new technology products or platforms want to be absorbed by users or audiences we need to have a corresponding purpose and application. Dr. Jonathan Crellin has aided students in producing ideas and slides for effective presentations, making the learning experience more confident for them. And educator Peter Farrell, who emphasized and added objective components to assist students to improve their ability to acquire information from a variety of sources.
Conclusion:
A technical essay needs to have a certain standard of specific purpose, organized content, key communication, and specific sources of information.

The third and fourth weeks are two special weeks of events such as Christmas and New Year. Research and the background factors related to it are the focus explored in topic 5. Students have approached topics about research thesis such as concept, structure, inductive reasoning, and inference. In addition, Dr. Jonathan Crellin brought "research paradigms" and research analysis methods to help students easily understand the 'fulcrum' to develop future research papers. Moreover, the professor also mentioned the introduction, the formula of "the data-ink ratio" and enhanced the knowledge of previous topics related to reference pages.
Feelings:
This is a topic that is new, and extremely important for students of engineering school. However, the learning atmosphere is not vibrant, and class activities are limited because the lesson plans are taught online. The knowledge is relatively difficult to grasp, and students need more effort to succeed. In addition, some students who have not understood the lesson still have the problem of being hesitant to ask the professor, and some small factors make online classes prevent students from absorbing the lesson. Nonetheless, Dr. Jonathan Crellin delivered the lesson very passionately and imparted the knowledge needed to the class to help students solve some of the difficulties.
Evaluation – Analysis:
The study statement's structure is painstakingly explained from the broad strokes to the specific criteria. Dr. Jonathan Crellin utilized comparisons to assist students to understand various facets of data structure and analysis, from objective to subjective, and the session was a success. Furthermore, he also introduced data analysis tools, specific visualizations, and differences in analytical research. Students' classroom success has been the acquisition of information that complements technical reports and provides confidence in bringing real proof to the table through the production of reference pages. What impressed me most about the lesson was the basis of "the value of data-ink ratio" and knowing the difference between inductive and deductive reasoning. "The value of data-ink ratio" helps me to be effective in drawing future analytical charts or maps, and reasoning methods help me be more thoughtful in writing research papers.
Conclusion:
The creation of research papers requires many basic elements that not only prescribe reference pages but also bring specific evidence from outside and provide detailed arguments. The selection of arguments from many aspects helps to increase the persuasiveness of the research. Moreover, it is very important to give the research legitimacy because it will bring professionalism to the research.
